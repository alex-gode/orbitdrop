Orbit Drop privacy policy

Last updated: 14 July 2026

Orbit Drop is built to keep to itself. It has no accounts, no sign-up, and no analytics of its own. Your runs, bests, streaks, settings, and cosmetic choices are stored only on your device.

What stays on your device

Almost everything. Statistics, settings, and cosmetic choices live in local storage and never leave your phone. The one exception is Game Center, which is on by default and shares your scores and achievements with Apple (see below); you can turn it off. Clearing local stats in Settings erases the on-device data; it does not affect your purchases, which Apple keeps against your Apple Account.

Game Center (on by default, optional)

Game Center is on by default so leaderboards and achievements work from the start. While it is on, your scores and unlocked achievements are shared with Apple's Game Center, handled by Apple under Apple's own privacy policy.

The Compete screen shows a leaderboard: your Game Center nickname, your photo if you have one, and your score, next to those of your Game Center friends who also play Orbit Drop. Apple supplies all of it, and only to you. The game does not store any of it, does not write it to your device, does not put it on a share card, and does not send it anywhere. It is drawn on screen and forgotten when you leave.

We never see your real name or your Apple Account: a Game Center nickname is whatever you chose to be called, and Apple decides who counts as a friend. Turning Game Center off in Settings stops all of it, and every part of the game still works.

Notifications (on by default)

Play reminders are on by default and scheduled entirely on your device. At first launch they are turned on quietly: they arrive in Notification Centre with no prompt and no alert until you choose to promote them, and you can turn them off in Settings at any time. There is no server and no push service; nothing about your play is ever sent anywhere to deliver them.

Purchases

Purchases are one-time and handled entirely by Apple's App Store. The app never sees your payment details. It only asks the App Store whether you own a product, so it can unlock it.

Advertising

This app shows ads. The ad provider may collect data (for example a device advertising identifier) to serve and measure ads, under the ad provider's own privacy policy. Buying Remove Ads removes the banner. The optional watch-an-ad revive is only ever shown when you choose it. See the ad provider's privacy policy for details of what it collects.

Your choices about ads

Where consent is required, the app asks before ads use your data, and you can change that decision at any time under Settings, Ad Privacy. Apple's tracking permission is separate and lives in iOS Settings, under Privacy and Security, then Tracking.

Children

The game does not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children.

Changes

If this policy changes, the updated version will appear here and in the App Store listing.

Contact

Reach the developer through the app's App Store listing.
